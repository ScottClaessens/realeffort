In Stage 2, you have been placed in a group of two workers. You will complete addition questions again, as in Stage 1, but your payoff depends on completing successful 'response cycles' rather than individual responses. A response cycle is successfully completed when your group correctly answers three questions in a row.

Addition questions are passed from group member to group member, in a kind of chain. The answer from the previous member is used in the question for the next group member. For example:

  1. Player 1 sees the addition question 50 + 60. They answer 110.
  2. Player 2 sees the addition question 110 + 40. They answer 150.
  3. Player 1 sees the addition question 150 + 20. They answers 170.

As you can see, each answer is used as the first number in the subsequent question in the chain. In the above example, all three questions were correctly answered, so the group would successfully complete the response cycle. However, if either group member incorrectly answers a question, the chain is broken, and the response cycle is not successful.

You will earn {{30|c}} for every 2 response cycles your group completes.

Stage 2 will consist of six rounds. In these rounds, you will experience each possible chain (i.e. ABA-BAB or BAB-ABA) three times. Your group will be timed in each round, as before.
