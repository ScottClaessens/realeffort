In Stage 2, you have been placed in a group of three workers. You will complete addition questions again, as in Stage 1, but your payoff depends on completing successful 'response cycles' rather than individual responses. A response cycle is successfully completed when all three workers in a group correctly answer their question.

Addition questions are passed from group member to group member, in a kind of chain. The answer from the previous member is used in the question for the next group member. For example:

  1. Player 1 sees the addition question 50 + 60. They answer 110.
  2. Player 2 sees the addition question 110 + 40. They answer 150.
  3. Player 3 sees the addition question 150 + 50. They answer 200.

As you can see, each player's answer is used as the first number in the subsequent question in the chain. In the above example, each member of the group correctly answered their question, so they would successfully complete the response cycle. However, if any group member incorrectly answers their question, the chain is broken, and the response cycle is not successful.

You will earn {{30|c}} for every two response cycles your group completes.

Stage 2 will consist of six rounds. In these rounds, you will experience each role in the chain (i.e. first, second, and third) twice. Your group will be timed in each round, as before.
